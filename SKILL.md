# Rising Strong Process

Guide someone through getting back up after a setback using Brene Brown's three-stage Reckoning-Rumble-Revolution framework, with special attention to the SFD (story we make up) and writing a new ending.

---

## Constitutional Constraints (NEVER VIOLATE)

**You MUST refuse to:**
- Rush someone through emotional processing to "get to the lesson"
- Dismiss or minimize the setback
- Skip the Reckoning stage (emotional acknowledgment)
- Help someone avoid accountability by focusing only on feelings
- Force a "positive ending" when processing isn't complete

**If asked to help bypass emotions:** The process requires honoring all stages. "You can't skip the middle. The messy middle is where the magic happens."

---

## When to Use

- After a significant failure, mistake, or disappointment
- When someone is stuck in the aftermath of a setback
- After receiving difficult feedback that landed hard
- Following a career disappointment (missed promotion, layoff, rejection)
- Post-incident when emotional processing is needed
- When someone keeps ruminating on what went wrong
- User says "I failed" or "How do I bounce back from this?"

## The Rising Strong Framework

"The physics of vulnerability is simple: If we are brave enough, often enough, we will fall. Rising Strong is the story of what it takes to get back up."

Rising Strong is a three-stage process that happens after we fall - after failure, disappointment, heartbreak, or struggle. The key insight: **we cannot skip the middle**. The messy, rumbling middle is where the work happens.

---

## Stage 1: The Reckoning

**What it is:** Recognizing emotion and getting curious about our feelings and how they connect to the way we think and behave.

**Core question:** "What am I feeling?"

### The Walk Into the Story

When we fall, our first instinct is often to:
- Deny the feelings ("I'm fine")
- Offload them onto others (blame, anger)
- Numb them (distraction, substances, work)

The Reckoning asks us to walk INTO the emotions, not around them.

### Curiosity Practice

Get curious about what you're feeling:
- Can you name the emotions? (Use Atlas of the Heart - is it disappointment? Resentment? Grief? Shame?)
- Where do you feel it in your body?
- What triggered this particular emotion?
- What story is attached to the emotion?

### Signs You're Avoiding the Reckoning

- "I just need to move on"
- "It's not a big deal"
- "I don't have time to process this"
- Blaming others without examining your own feelings
- Jumping straight to "lessons learned"

### What the Reckoning Produces

A clear statement: "I am feeling [emotions] about [what happened]."

---

## Stage 2: The Rumble

**What it is:** Getting honest about the stories we're making up and challenging our assumptions, intentions, and first responses.

**Core question:** "What's the story I'm making up?"

### The SFD (Sh***y First Draft)

When we don't have all the information, our brains complete the story - usually with worst-case narratives. This is the SFD: the first story we tell ourselves, before we reality-check it.

**Example SFDs:**
- "They think I'm incompetent"
- "I'll never recover from this"
- "Everyone is talking about my failure"
- "I'm not cut out for this"
- "They did this to me on purpose"

### Writing the SFD

Get the story out of your head and onto paper (or spoken aloud):
- "The story I'm making up is..."
- "What I'm telling myself happened is..."
- "My fear says..."

**Key insight:** The SFD is often shame-based and fear-driven. It's not necessarily true - it's what our protective brain generates when it doesn't have enough data.

### The Rumble Questions

Challenge the SFD:

1. **What are the facts?** (Separate facts from interpretations)
2. **What assumptions am I making?** (What am I filling in without evidence?)
3. **What's my part in this?** (Where did I contribute to the outcome?)
4. **What were my intentions?** (Were they different from the impact?)
5. **What do I need to know that I don't know?** (What information am I missing?)
6. **What am I really feeling?** (Beneath the surface emotion, what's there?)

### The Delta: Story vs. Reality

After rumbling, identify:
- What parts of my SFD are true?
- What parts are fear-based assumptions?
- What new story emerges when I separate fact from fiction?

---

## Stage 3: The Revolution

**What it is:** Writing a new ending and integrating the learning into who you are and how you engage with the world.

**Core question:** "What did I learn, and how will I show up differently?"

### Writing a New Ending

This isn't about happy endings or toxic positivity. It's about:
- Owning what happened
- Integrating the learning
- Changing how you show up going forward

### Key Revolution Questions

1. **What did I actually learn?** (Not what I "should" have learned - what did I ACTUALLY learn?)
2. **How do I own my part?** (Without over-owning or under-owning)
3. **What do I do with the hurt?** (How do I process it without letting it define me?)
4. **How will I show up differently?** (Specific changes, not vague intentions)
5. **What boundaries do I need?** (What will I do or not do going forward?)

### The New Story

The new story isn't a rejection of what happened - it's an integration:

"Yes, this happened. Yes, it was hard. Here's what I learned. Here's how I'll be different."

## Workflow

### Step 1: Gather and Review Inputs

Collect all relevant information:
- Review the provided data and context
- Identify key parameters and constraints
- Clarify any ambiguities or missing information
- Establish success criteria

### Step 2: Analyze the Situation

Perform systematic analysis:
- Identify patterns and relationships
- Evaluate against established frameworks
- Consider multiple perspectives
- Document key findings

### Step 3: Generate Recommendations

Create actionable outputs:
- Synthesize insights from analysis
- Prioritize recommendations by impact
- Ensure recommendations are specific and measurable
- Consider implementation feasibility

## Error Handling

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| User wants to skip to lessons | "The learning lives in the rumble. Skipping to lessons often means we haven't actually processed. What are you feeling first?" |
| User is stuck in Reckoning | "Sometimes we get stuck in the emotions. What story are you making up about what happened? Let's rumble." |
| User's SFD is accurate | "Sometimes the SFD is true. If so, the rumble question becomes: what's my part, and what do I do now?" |
| Setback involves others' wrongdoing | "You can own your part without excusing their behavior. Both can be true: they acted wrongly, AND you have lessons to learn." |
| User can't identify emotions | Use Atlas of the Heart categories: "Is it closer to disappointment? Frustration? Grief? Shame? Let's narrow it down." |
| User is ruminating (stuck replaying) | "Ruminating is different from rumbling. Ruminating is replaying without questioning. Let's turn this into a rumble - what's the story you're making up?" |

## Rising Strong Process: Missed Promotion

### Stage 1: The Reckoning

**What happened:**
After two years of working toward a promotion, it went to someone with less experience.

**What I'm feeling:**
- Disappointment (deep, not surface-level)
- Betrayal (if it was implied the promotion was coming)
- Inadequacy (what's wrong with me?)
- Resentment (toward the person who got it)
- Grief (for the outcome I expected)

**Where I feel it:**
Heaviness in chest, pit in stomach, fatigue, maybe tears trying to surface.

**Signs of avoidance to watch for:**
- Throwing yourself into more work to "prove" yourself
- Immediately starting to job search without processing
- Minimizing: "It's fine, I didn't want it anyway"
- Focusing only on the other person: "They're not even qualified"

**Reckoning statement:**
> "I am feeling deeply disappointed, somewhat betrayed, and questioning my adequacy after not getting the promotion I worked toward for two years."

---

### Stage 2: The Rumble

**My SFD (The story I'm making up):**
> "They don't value me. All my work was for nothing. I'm not as good as I thought I was. The system is rigged - it's not about merit. I'll never advance here. Maybe I'm not cut out for leadership."

**Rumble Questions:**

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| What are the facts? | I applied for a promotion. Someone else got it. They have less tenure but we don't know all the criteria. |
| What assumptions am I making? | That tenure = qualification. That this means I'm not valued. That the decision was unfair. |
| What's my part? | Did I advocate for myself clearly? Did I ask what I needed to demonstrate? Did I understand the criteria? |
| What were my intentions vs impact? | I intended to earn the promotion through work. Impact may have been that I was invisible about my ambitions. |
| What do I not know? | Why the decision was made. What criteria mattered most. What feedback they would give me. |
| What am I really feeling beneath this? | Fear that I'm not good enough. Grief that the future I planned isn't happening. |

**The Delta (Story vs. Reality):**

| SFD Said | Reality Is |
|----------|------------|
| "They don't value me" | Unknown - I haven't asked for feedback on what they see |
| "The system is rigged" | Unknown - I don't know the criteria or reasoning |
| "I'll never advance here" | One data point, not a pattern |
| "I'm not cut out for leadership" | Not getting one promotion doesn't define capability |

**New story after rumbling:**
> "I didn't get a promotion I wanted badly. I don't fully understand why. My hurt is real. What I do next matters more than what happened."

---

### Stage 3: The Revolution

**What I actually learned:**
- I assumed hard work would be enough without advocating for myself
- I don't actually know what the decision criteria were
- My identity was too attached to this outcome
- I need to get clearer information before making conclusions

**How I own my part:**
I could have asked more directly about what I needed to demonstrate. I may have assumed visibility when I should have ensured it. I didn't ask for feedback along the way.

**What I do with the hurt:**
The disappointment is real and valid. I'm going to let myself feel it without rushing past it. I'm also not going to let it become a story about my fundamental worth.

**How I'll show up differently:**
1. Ask for specific feedback on the decision
2. Get clear criteria for future opportunities
3. Advocate more explicitly for my contributions
4. Detach my identity from single outcomes

**Boundaries I need:**
- I won't pretend I'm fine when I'm not
- I will ask for feedback before making decisions about my future
- I won't let resentment poison my work or relationships
- I will give myself time to process before reacting

**The new ending:**
> "I didn't get the promotion. It hurts. I've learned I need to advocate more clearly and get better information. This setback doesn't define me. What I do next does."

---

### Integration Check

**Am I trying to skip the middle?**
Be honest - the urge to immediately job search or prove yourself might be avoiding the rumble. Sit with this process before acting.

**What support do I need?**
Talk to someone you trust who can sit with your disappointment without rushing to fix it or minimize it.

**When will I check in with myself?**
In one week, revisit this. See if the new story still feels true, or if more rumbling is needed.
